LoreTracker is published under the **GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPL-3.0)** (see `LICENSE`). The AGPL lets anyone use, modify, self-host and even commercially deploy LoreTracker **for free** — on one condition: if you convey it or run a modified version as a network service, you must make the complete corresponding source code available to your users under the AGPL.

**A separate commercial license is available** for cases where you cannot or do not want to meet the AGPL's copyleft (source-disclosure) obligations, for example:

- embedding LoreTracker (or a derivative) into **proprietary / closed-source** software
- offering a **hosted / SaaS** service based on a modified LoreTracker **without** publishing your source changes
- any distribution where releasing your modifications under the AGPL is not an option for you

The commercial license grants an exemption from the AGPL's copyleft requirements on negotiated terms. This is a standard open-core / dual-licensing model: the project stays fully Free Software under the AGPL, and the copyright holder funds development by selling copyleft exceptions.

A commercial license is only relevant when AGPL copyleft does not fit your situation. If you are unsure, just ask; we'd rather have a quick email exchange up front than discover a misunderstanding later.
